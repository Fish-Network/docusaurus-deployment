1) **Collaboration**

Capital markets are entering a structural transition. For decades, investment activity has been organized around centralized intermediaries: fund managers, custodians, administrators, and compliance firms. While digital infrastructure has improved execution and reporting, the core architecture of capital formation remains largely unchanged.

A new market structure is now emerging: capital coordination systems. These systems treat capital pools, legal entities, compliance workflows, custody, and liquidity as programmable infrastructure rather than organizational overhead. The result is a model where collaboration itself becomes the organizing unit of investment

Fish Network represents this shift. It introduces a framework in which groups can form legally compliant, asset-holding entities; pool capital; execute investments; manage liquidity; and distribute returns using deterministic workflows. Rather than relying on hierarchical management, it enables coordination through shared infrastructure.

In this model:

● Funds become software systems.
● Compliance becomes an embedded feature.
● Custody becomes native to identity.
● Liquidity becomes structured rather than speculative.
● Collaboration becomes a financial primitive.
 
 This paper outlines the emergence of decentralized capital coordination and its implications for financial markets.
 
The Market Problem

Traditional investment structures were designed for a pre-digital world. They assume:

● Centralized management authority
● Manual compliance workflows
● Fragmented custody systems
● High operational overhead
● Limited participation due to complexity and cost

These constraints limit experimentation, slow capital formation, and concentrate financial power into a small number of large institutions.

At the same time, crypto infrastructure has solved important technical problems:

● Digital custody
● Tokenization
● Settlement
● Transparency

But it has not solved coordination. Most crypto systems still rely on speculative markets or informal DAOs, neither of which provide the legal, compliance, and financial rigor required for institutional capital.

The New Market Structure

Capital coordination systems introduce a new type of financial object: the programmable capital pool.

Rather than organizing investment activity around:

 ● A manager
● A fund brand
● A closed LP structure
They organize it around:
● A legally instantiated entity
● A treasury
● Embedded compliance
● Tokenized membership
● Deterministic capital flows
This transforms the architecture of investing from organizational hierarchy to system design.

Liquidity Without Exchanges

Liquidity is designed as part of the system rather than outsourced to markets.
